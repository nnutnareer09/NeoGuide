Prepare the following information before opening the calculator
  • Gestational age at birth (weeks + days)
  • Highest intrapartum maternal temperature (°C or °F)
  • Duration of rupture of membranes (hours)
  • Maternal GBS status (positive / negative / unknown)
  • Intrapartum antibiotic prophylaxis given (type + timing)
  • Infant's clinical appearance at time of assessment (Well / Equivocal / Ill)
Classification of Infant's Clinical Presentation
Clinical Exam Description
Clinical Illness
  1. Persistent need for NCPAP / HFNC / mechanical ventilation (outside of the delivery room)
  2. Hemodynamic instability requiring vasoactive drugs
  3. Neonatal encephalopathy / Perinatal depression
    • Seizure
    • Apgar Score @ 5 minutes < 5
  4. Need for supplemental O₂ ≥ 2 hours to maintain oxygen saturations > 90% (outside of the delivery room)
Equivocal
  1. Persistent physiologic abnormality ≥ 4 hrs:
    • Tachycardia (HR ≥ 160)
    • Tachypnea (RR ≥ 60)
    • Temperature instability (≥ 100.4°F or < 97.5°F)
    • Respiratory distress (grunting, flaring, or retracting) not requiring supplemental O₂
  2. Two or more physiologic abnormalities lasting ≥ 2 hrs:
    • Tachycardia (HR ≥ 160)
    • Tachypnea (RR ≥ 60)
    • Temperature instability (≥ 100.4°F or < 97.5°F)
    • Respiratory distress (grunting, flaring, or retracting) not requiring supplemental O₂
Note: abnormality can be intermittent
Well Appearing No persistent physiologic abnormalities

All of the above + jaundice in first 24 h · TcB/TSB rise ≥ 0.3 mg/dL/h (first 24 h) or ≥ 0.2 mg/dL/h after · Down syndrome · exclusive breastfeeding with > 7% BW loss · family history of phototherapy · bruising / cephalhematoma · IDM + macrosomia ≥ 4,000 g
