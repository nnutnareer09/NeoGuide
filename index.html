Classification of Infant's Clinical Presentation
Clinical Exam Description
Clinical Illness
  1. Persistent need for NCPAP / HFNC / mechanical ventilation (outside of the delivery room)
  2. Hemodynamic instability requiring vasoactive drugs
  3. Neonatal encephalopathy / Perinatal depression
    • Seizure
    • Apgar Score @ 5 minutes < 5
  4. Need for supplemental O₂ ≥ 2 hours to maintain oxygen saturations > 90% (outside of the delivery room)
Equivocal
  1. Persistent physiologic abnormality ≥ 4 hrs:
    • Tachycardia (HR ≥ 160)
    • Tachypnea (RR ≥ 60)
    • Temperature instability (≥ 100.4°F or < 97.5°F)
    • Respiratory distress (grunting, flaring, or retracting) not requiring supplemental O₂
  2. Two or more physiologic abnormalities lasting ≥ 2 hrs:
    • Tachycardia (HR ≥ 160)
    • Tachypnea (RR ≥ 60)
    • Temperature instability (≥ 100.4°F or < 97.5°F)
    • Respiratory distress (grunting, flaring, or retracting) not requiring supplemental O₂
Note: abnormality can be intermittent
Well Appearing No persistent physiologic abnormalities

Total exchange transfusion
Volume = 2 × blood volume (mL/kg) × weight (kg)
Blood volume: 80 mL/kg (term) · 90 mL/kg (preterm)
Composition: LPBI 2/3 + FFP 1/3 · Vol per cycle: 3–5 mL/kg · Duration: 60–90 min · Route: UVC
Blood type: LPBI group O + FFP group AB (or per cross-match)
Partial exchange transfusion
Polycythemia (dilute with NSS):
Vol (mL) = (Hct_obs − Hct_desired) ÷ Hct_obs × blood volume
Severe anemia (with LPBI):
Vol (mL) = (Hct_desired − Hct_obs) ÷ (Hct_LPBI − Hct_obs) × blood volume
Hct desired = 50–55% · Hct LPBI ≈ 70%

Norepinephrine (Noradrenaline)

💓 Norepinephrine Neofax 2025
Brand: Levophed  ·  Class: Vasopressor / Catecholamine
Dose — Hypotension / Septic Shock
Start: 0.05–0.5 mcg/kg/min IV continuous infusion
Titrate: 0.05–0.2 mcg/kg/min q15–30 min to goal BP
Usual max: 1–2 mcg/kg/min
Reported range in neonates < 34 wk: 0.02–7.1 mcg/kg/min
Median dose for shock reversal at 30–60 min: 0.3–0.5 mcg/kg/min
First-line agent for septic shock per Surviving Sepsis Campaign (alongside epinephrine). Start after 40–60 mL/kg fluid resuscitation, or sooner if fluid overload.

Neonatal screening

CCHD pulse oximetry screening Pediatrics 2020
Measure right hand (RH) + one foot (F) at ~24 h of age (or earlier if discharging)
1
Fail immediately if SpO₂ ≤ 89% in RH or F → notify cardiologist
2
Retest in 1 hour if 90–94% in RH or F, or ≥ 4% difference between RH & F
3
Pass if ≥ 95% in both RH and F, and ≤ 3% difference between sites
4
After retest — Fail if ≤ 94% in RH or F, or ≥ 4% difference → notify cardiologist for CCHD evaluation
